Overview - Automobile engineering

Do you love roaring red Ferraris, loud and large Ducatis, or other vehicles for that matter? Not just how they look, but how they function, what makes them go as fast as they do? Do you have a desire to innovate the automotive industry, like a member of a Mercedes Formula One team, constantly pushing the envelope to stay ahead of the competition? Finally, do you love numbers, be it in Maths or Physics? Then automotive engineering just might be the course for you. Read on as we fast track you into the world of automotive engineering.

Skills and Personality

Just like the cars / bikes / vehicles you’ll be studying about, it takes a few components put together to make a good engineer. Do you posses any of these skills ?

Good with Numbers

Automotive engineering requires, you need to love numbers as much as you do cars. While watching engineers on Formula 1 teams work on the cars is a cool sight to behold, the amount of dedication towards numbers is what allowed them to get to where they are.

Good with Your hands

Say your close friend’s car breaks down. While you don’t know the exact component that malfunctioned, you know how to fix it, and you proceed to do it well, so much so, the car starts running like new. You need to be ready to get your hands dirty.

Problem-Solver

Automotive engineers are problem solvers. They use math skills and analytical abilities to spot problems and resolve them. When something isn't working, they analyse the issue and find a solution faster than you can blink.

Good Communicator

You’re capable of speaking in more than 2 languages, perhaps something you picked up from hanging out with your friends of different races. Furthermore, you tend to relay any message, maybe from a teacher to the rest of the class in a clear and easily understood manner.

Becoming Professional

Requirements to become a Professional Engineer in Malaysia
1 Becoming a Professional Engineer in Malaysia Through the Board of Engineers Malaysia (BEM)

A fresh graduate is encouraged to register with the IEM as a graduate engineer. Not only will you be kept up to date with the latest developments in engineering, you are also entitled to the title Engr. once you do. It takes at least 3 years before being eligible to apply for Corporate Membership with the Institution of Engineers, Malaysia (IEM) and subsequent Professional Engineer registration with the Board of Engineers Malaysia (BEM). In order to be able to sit for the Professional Interview, applicants SHOULD have at least 3 years of working experience. Once you have sat and passed the Professional Interview, you can then proceed to obtain the Ir (P.Eng) from the Board of Engineers Malaysia (BEM).

IF you have registered with BEM and have three years of working experience, according to their clock, you can get your P.Eng. immediately. If you HAVEN’T, register with them and wait three years before getting your P.Eng.

Going Overseas

Thinking about going out of the country to work once you’ve become a professional ? Well, the good news is that Malaysia is part of the Washington Accord, an agreement signed by various countries, which basically means that graduates of accredited programmes in any of the signatory countries are recognised by the other signatories as having met the academic requirements for entry to the practice of engineering.
